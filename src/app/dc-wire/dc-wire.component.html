DC-Wire

Here are some basic facts about direct current (DC) wires:

DC wires are power cables that transmit and distribute direct current. They are used in many applications, including renewable energy systems, electric vehicles, and portable electronics.      How they work DC wires have a simple construction with positive and negative poles. They are designed to have a soft multi-strand core, good electrical conductivity, and heat resistance. How they compare to AC wires DC wires are generally more flexible and easier to install than alternating current (AC) wires.

    They are also more resistant to voltage drop and have lower line losses. How they are used DC wires are used in many applications, including: Solar photovoltaic panels Wind turbines Electric vehicles Portable electronics Household appliances Consumer electronics How they are connected DC cables require a converter station at both ends of the link to convert direct current into alternating current and vice versa. How they are installed DC cables can be used for flexible and fixed installations indoors and outdoors. They are easy to install and maintain. DC wiring refers to the system of electric wiring that is designed to carry direct current (DC) electrical power. Direct current is the flow of electric charge in one particular direction, whereas alternating current (AC) changes direction periodically.

DC power cables (direct current transmission) are designed with a soft multi-strand core, good electrical conductivity, heat resistance, and usually have two insulation, and do not have surface effects like alternating current.
Solar DC Wires

Solar DC cables have many specifications, including:

Voltage

The voltage rating for a solar DC cable may be 1.5 (1.8) KV DC or 0.6 / 1.0 (1.2) KV AC.

Temperature

The ambient temperature for a solar DC cable may be -40°C to +90°C. The maximum conductor temperature may be 120°C.

Resistance

Solar DC cables should be resistant to weather, UV rays, mineral oils, chemicals, acids, and alkaline.

Bending radius

The minimum bending radius for a solar DC cable may be 4 times the overall diameter.

Solar DC Cable Identification

For DC (direct current) wire identification:


Pulling force:

The safe pulling force for a solar DC cable may be -50 N/sqmm.

Insulation resistance:

The insulation resistance for a solar DC cable may be 1000 MΩ/km.

Spark test

The spark test for a solar DC cable may be 6000 V AC(8000 V DC). The size of the solar DC cable you need depends on the solar panel rating and the distance between the panels and loads.
