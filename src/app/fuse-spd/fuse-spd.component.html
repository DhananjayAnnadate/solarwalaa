An MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker) is an automatically operated electrical switch designed to protect an electrical circuit from damage caused by overload or short circuits. It is a safer and more reliable alternative to traditional fuses.

A single-pole MCB is designed to protect a single-phase circuit (one live wire) from overcurrent conditions, such as overloads and short circuits.
It disconnects the live wire automatically when the current exceeds the rated value.

An auxiliary contact is an additional component that attaches to a main circuit breaker (MCB) or residual current device (RCD/RCCB).
Its primary function is to provide a secondary signaling function or control, such as remote monitoring, switching, or status indication of the main circuit breaker.
